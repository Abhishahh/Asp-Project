Introduction

Computers can help us with a wide range of problems, but in order for this to happen, we need to undertake a 2-step process: Formulate the precise sequence of steps needed to solve the problem. This is known as an algorithm. Give the algorithm to a computer in a language it understands, so that it can perform the task(s) we give it. This is known as coding. The process of creating algorithms and turning them into code is called programming.

Glossary

  1. Coding - the process of assigning a code to something for classification or identification.
  2. Programming - the process or activity of writing computer programs.
  3. Difference - a point or way in which people or things are dissimilar.
  4. Content - a thing that is explained
  5. History - the study of past events, particularly in human affairs.
  6. Algorithms - a process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations, especially by a computer.
  7. Skill - the ability to do something well
  8. Create - bring (something) into existence
  9. Website - a set of related web pages located under a single domain name, typically produced by a single person or organization.
  10. learn - gain or acquire knowledge of or skill in (something) by study, experience, or being taught.
  11. Career - an occupation undertaken for a significant period of a person's life and with opportunities for progress.
  12. Invention - the action of inventing something, typically a process or device.
  13. Code - a system of words, letters, figures, or symbols used to represent others, especially for the purposes of secrecy.
  14. Deepfake - a video of a person in which their face or body has been digitally altered so that they appear to be someone else, typically used maliciously or to spread false information.
  15. Practical - of or concerned with the actual doing or use of something rather than with theory and ideas.
  16. conclusion - the end or finish of an event, process, or text.
  17. Relevance - the quality or state of being closely connected or appropriate.
  18. disinformation - false information which is intended to mislead, especially propaganda issued by a government organization to a rival power or the media.
  19. Automation - the use or introduction of automatic equipment in a manufacturing or other process or facility.
